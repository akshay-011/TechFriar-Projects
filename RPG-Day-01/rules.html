Requirements for password


  • Make your password at least 10-16 characters long. Longer passwords are generally more secure.
  • Use a mix of character types, including uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters (such as !, @, #, $, etc.).
  • Avoid using easily guessable words, like "password," "123456," or your name. Dictionary words and common phrases are vulnerable to dictionary attacks.
  • Avoid using easily discoverable information such as your username, real name, or company name in your password.
  • Use a mixture of random characters that don't form a recognizable pattern.
  • Use different passwords for different accounts. Reusing passwords across multiple accounts increases your vulnerability if one account is compromised.
